About the University

A university (Latin: universitas, 'a whole') is an institution of higher (or tertiary) education and research which awards academic degrees in several academic disciplines. Universities typically offer both undergraduate and postgraduate programs. The word university is derived from the Latin universitas magistrorum et scholarium, which roughly means "community of teachers and scholars".[1] The first universities were created in Europe by Catholic Church monks.
